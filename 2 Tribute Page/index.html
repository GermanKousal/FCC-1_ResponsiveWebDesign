A tribute to Alan Turing

The man who saved the world

Here's a timeline of Turing's life:

  1. Jun 23, 1912: Alan Mathison Turing was born on June 23, 1912 in Maida Vale, London.
  2. Aug 19, 1926: At the age of 13, Alan Turing first showed signs of intelligence and potential which was noticed at the Sherborne School.
  3. Aug 29, 1931: Alan Turing studied from 1931 to 1934 at King's College, in Cambridge. He was first in his class and was an honor student in the field of mathematics.
  4. Sep 30, 1936: Turing studied mathematics and cryptology while also building three of four stages of an electro-mechanical binary multiplier at Princeton University.
  5. Dec 6, 1939: Turing helped to solve the essential part of the German naval indicator system and also came up with the idea of sequential analysis.
  6. Mar 18, 1940: Turing was responsible for creating the Bombe which was a cryptanalytic machine used to decrypt the Enigma-enciphered messages being sent from the enemies.
  7. Nov 29, 1942: Turing traveled to the United States to work with the US naval cryptanalysts to create their version of the Bombe and an Enigma in Washington.
  8. Feb 19, 1946: He presented his paper in February of 1946 where he had presented the first detailed design of a stored program computer setup.
  9. Sep 13, 1947: Turing lived in Hampton, London where worked on the design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine).
  10. Mar 19, 1948: Turing created the LU decomposition method that was used and is still used today to solve matrix equations.
  11. Aug 31, 1948: Turing was appointed a position in the mathematics department at the University of Manchester where he worked on software for one of the earliest stored-program computers.
  12. Apr 29, 1950: The turing test was created in 1950. The turing test was created to compare the intelligence of a machine/computer to the intelligence of a human brain.
  13. Jan 29, 1952: Turing was caught in a relationship with an unemployed 19 year old man. He was convicted for indecency because homosexual acts were considered criminal offences in the United Kingdom at the time. This is what lead to his downhill spiral right before his suicide.
  14. Aug 14, 1952: Turing's book "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" was published in 1952 which was all about his interest in the biology of organisms.
  15. Jun 8, 1954: Turing committed suicide in his house where his housekeeper found him dead.

A computer would deserve to be called intelligent if it could deceive a human into believing that it was human.

--- Alan Turing
